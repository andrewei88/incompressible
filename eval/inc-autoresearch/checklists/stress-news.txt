1. At 18:32 Washington time, Trump posted that the US and Iran were "very far along" with a "definitive" peace agreement and he had agreed to a two-week ceasefire, just before the 20:00 EDT strike deadline
2. The ceasefire is contingent on Iran suspending hostilities and fully opening the Strait of Hormuz to commercial shipping
3. After the announcement, oil dropped below $100 per barrel for the first time in days and US stock futures soared
4. Tuesday morning Trump had threatened the death of Iranian civilisation, "never to be brought back again"
5. Iranian foreign minister Seyed Aragchi said Iran would halt "defensive operations" and allow safe passage through Hormuz "via coordination with Iran's armed forces"
6. Iran's 10-point plan: US withdraw military from the region, lift sanctions, pay compensation for war damages, Iran maintains control over Hormuz
7. Democrats condemned Trump (Joaquin Castro called him unfit, Chuck Schumer pressured Republicans); Republicans Austin Scott, Ron Johnson, Nathaniel Moran, and Lisa Murkowski broke with him over the civilisation threat
8. Iran's military has been significantly degraded and many top leaders killed in strikes, but the regime is still in power and the disposition of enriched uranium is unknown
9. Partial political victory for Trump — but "a reprieve, not a permanent settlement"
10. Trump's words "may have fundamentally altered the way the rest of the world views the US"
