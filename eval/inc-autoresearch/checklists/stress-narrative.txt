1. Orwell was sub-divisional police officer in Moulmein, lower Burma, "hated by large numbers of people"
2. Divided mind: knew imperialism was an evil thing he should quit, yet "the greatest joy in the world would be to drive a bayonet into a Buddhist priest's guts"
3. A tame elephant in "must" with broken chain had ravaged the bazaar; its mahout was twelve hours away
4. Rounding a hut, Orwell found the dead body of an Indian Dravidian coolie the elephant had ground into the earth
5. The crowd grew to at least two thousand, following him because the shooting would be "a bit of fun" and they wanted the meat
6. Orwell knew with perfect certainty he ought not to shoot — the elephant was peacefully eating, worth ~100 pounds alive vs only ~5 pounds dead for its tusks
7. He realized he had to shoot because "I could feel their two thousand wills pressing me forward, irresistibly"
8. Thesis: he "first grasped the hollowness, the futility of the white man's dominion in the East"; the white man becomes "a hollow, posing dummy" who "wears a mask, and his face grows to fit it"
9. Fear: a white man mustn't be frightened before natives; the sole thought was being trampled and laughed at — "one long struggle not to be laughed at"
10. Final line: "I often wondered whether any of the others grasped that I had done it solely to avoid looking a fool"
