ARGUMENT-PARALLEL · Key Points

The Grift

  • The "Chief AI Transformation Officer" role is 11 months old. The job: buy software nobody asked for and measure whether people use it. They never use it. Three promotions.
  • $14.2M spent on AI tools last year. Selection criteria: a 40-page matrix, three vendor dinners, and a 2024 Gartner printout taped to the wall. Two of the four quadrant leaders no longer exist.
  • 95% of AI pilots fail to deliver measurable ROI. This stat is in the same Gartner report. He didn't print that page.

ARGUMENT-SEQUENTIAL · Chain

The Metrics Loop

Build "AI Fluency Index" from login frequency + training quiz + undefined "AI-forward mindset" rating → managers don't understand rubric, give everyone 3/5 → report "78% meet expectations" to board → nobody is fluent, but the number exists → number justifies budget → budget funds more tools → tools generate more logins → logins are "adoption"

ARGUMENT-PARALLEL · Key Points

Weaponized Anxiety

  • 57% of employees report AI replacement anxiety. He commissioned the survey, then put the number on slide 6 under "Urgency Indicators." Their fear is his business case.
  • A senior analyst presented a time study showing 11 min/day net productivity loss from autocomplete. He forwarded it to HR as "change resistance." She received a meeting titled "Aligning with Organizational Transformation Priorities." She stopped presenting time studies.
  • Training quiz includes: "True or false: AI can help improve your daily workflow." The answer is true. It was true before the tools existed.
ARGUMENT-SEQUENTIAL · Chain

The Inversion

Workers fear AI will replace them → but the AI summarizes emails by repeating them and autocompletes "sounds good to me" → the real threat is the executive who bought the tools, made training mandatory, tied fluency to performance reviews, and labeled dissent as "resistance" → they are afraid of the wrong thing
