The Persistence Problem

  • Duke: "There are only two things that determine how your life turns out. One is luck, which we don't have any control over. And second... the quality of your decisions."
  • "For most people over 25 who are professionals, they are too gritty. They're sticking to things way too long." Duke cites Angela Duckworth's research on grit, then argues we've lost the ability to recognize when persistence becomes counterproductive.
  • We judge decisions by outcomes ("resulting") instead of process. A good decision can produce bad outcomes through luck, and a poor decision can work out through dumb luck.
  • The sunk-cost fallacy keeps people invested in failing paths. As a Japanese proverb puts it: "If you get on the wrong train, get off at the next station."
  • As journalist Sydney Harris wrote: "Perseverance is the most overrated of traits... beating your head against a wall is more likely to produce a concussion in the head than a hole in the wall."

Argument-Parallel Key Points

Decision Education

  • Duke: "We spend a lot of time teaching kids trigonometry... once we get past addition, subtraction, division, multiplication, how about just teaching people probabilities? Because the core of every single decision that you make is a forecast about the future."
  • This insight led Duke and her husband to found the Alliance for Decision Education in 2014, aiming to teach decision-making as a core skill alongside reading and math.
  • In classrooms where teachers implemented decision education programs, researchers observed improvements in math and science scores alongside reductions in disciplinary problems.
