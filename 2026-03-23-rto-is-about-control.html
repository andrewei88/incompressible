RTO Is About Control, Not Productivity

x.com (@PeterGirnus) · 1,120 → 195 words · 1 min · 5 min saved
ARGUMENT-PARALLEL · Key Points

The Real Purpose

  • RTO mandates are about executive control and visibility, not productivity or culture
  • "Serendipitous collisions" and "culture" are unfalsifiable justifications — chosen because they can't be measured or disproved
  • Executives who mandate RTO exempt themselves ("strategic leaders require location flexibility")
ARGUMENT-SEQUENTIAL · Chain

The Metrics Loop

Empty office is a cost liability → mandate RTO to justify $4.2M/yr lease → install $380K badge tracking → measure badge swipes as proxy for "culture" → report presence metrics to board → claim success despite zero productivity change

ARGUMENT-PARALLEL · Key Points

The Human Cost

  • 340 employees flagged "Open to Work" within days; 47 engineers quit month one — reframed as "natural attrition" and "alignment correction"
  • Productivity was up 23% remote but dismissed because "productivity isn't everything"
  • Remaining employees aren't loyal — they have mortgages, school districts, unvested RSUs. On paper it looks like retention.
  • Managers weaponize flexibility by tying personal leave requests to performance reviews
ARGUMENT-PARALLEL · Key Points

The Quiet Part

  • CEO was shown a stock photo as evidence RTO worked. Real occupancy: 40% on a good day.
  • Employees commute 45 min to take 7.4 Zoom calls/day they could take from home
  • "Hybrid" = 3 mandatory days + 2 days with mandatory "anchor meetings" that are "strongly encouraged" (mandatory without liability)
  • $2K "collaboration bonus" for badge compliance costs less than turnover — framing coercion as incentive
