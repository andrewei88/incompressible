Chronology · Timeline

Key Milestones

2006
  • Shinya Yamanaka shows 4 factors (OSKM) reprogram adult cells to iPSCs
2012
  • Yamanaka wins Nobel Prize (shared with John Gurdon)
2016
  • Belmonte lab shows partial reprogramming reverses aging in progeria mice
2020
  • Sinclair lab restores vision in old mice using OSK gene therapy
2024
  • OSK via AAV9 extends remaining lifespan by 109% in 124-week-old mice
2026
  • First clinical trial (ER-100 for optic neuropathies) planned by Life Biosciences

Critical Challenges

ChallengeDetail
Tissue variabilityDifferent cell types need different parameters. What benefits lung may cause cell death in liver.
Cancer riskProlonged exposure causes tumor formation, intestinal and liver failure, loss of cellular identity.
DeliveryNo current method for safe whole-body partial reprogramming. Most progress limited to isolated tissues (retina).
Dosing precisionMust be precisely timed (1 week on/off via doxycycline control). Too little = no effect. Too much = dedifferentiation.
Translation gapSizable hurdles between mouse rejuvenation and human clinical application.
